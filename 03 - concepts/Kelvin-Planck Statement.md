The Kelvin-Planck statement says that no heat engine operating in a [[Cyclic Transformation|cycle]] can have the extraction of heat from a single reservoir and the complete conversion of that heat into work as its sole effect.

It rules out a perfect [[Idealized Heat Engine|heat engine]] whose efficiency is equal to $1$.

It is equivalent to [[Clausius Statement|Clausius statement]].

First introduced in [[1.4 the Second Law]].
