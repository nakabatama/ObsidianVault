A Legendre transformation replaces a state function's dependence on a variable by dependence on its conjugate variable.

In thermodynamics, it converts [[Internal Energy|internal energy]] into potentials adapted to externally controlled constraints, including [[Enthalpy|enthalpy]], [[Helmholtz Free Energy|Helmholtz free energy]], [[Gibbs Free Energy|Gibbs free energy]], and the [[Grand Potential|grand potential]].

First introduced in [[1.7 Approach to Equilibrium and the Thermodynamic Potentials]].
