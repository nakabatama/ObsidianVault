The internal energy $E(\mathrm{X})$ is a [[State Function|state function]] of a thermodynamic system.

It can be defined by the work needed for an [[Adiabatic Transformation|adiabatic transformation]] from an initial state $\mathrm{X_i}$ to a final state $\mathrm{X_f}$:

$$
W=E(\mathrm{X_f})-E(\mathrm{X_i}).
$$

When the adiabatic constraint is removed, the difference between the change in internal energy and the work is defined as heat.
