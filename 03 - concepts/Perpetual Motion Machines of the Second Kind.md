Perpetual motion machines of the second kind produce work by converting heat from a single reservoir completely into work, without any other effect.

They do not violate the [[First Law of Thermodynamics|first law of thermodynamics]], but they are ruled out by the [[Second Law of Thermodynamics|second law of thermodynamics]].

First introduced at [[Kardar - 1.4 The Second Law]].
