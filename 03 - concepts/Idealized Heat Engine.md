An idealized heat engine takes heat $Q_H$ from a heat source, converts part of it into work $W$, and dumps the remaining heat into a heat sink.

Its efficiency is measured by the fraction of input heat converted into work:

$$
\eta=\frac{W}{Q_H}.
$$

The [[Kelvin-Planck Statement]] says that a perfect heat engine cannot convert heat from a single reservoir completely into work.

First introduced in [[1.4 the Second Law]].
