An idealized refrigerator uses work $W$ to extract heat $Q_C$ from a cold system and dumps heat $Q_H$ into a hotter system.

Its performance is measured by the figure of merit:

$$
\omega=\frac{Q_C}{W}=\frac{Q_C}{Q_H-Q_C}.
$$

The [[Clausius Statement|Clausius statement]] says that heat cannot be transferred from a colder body to a hotter body as the sole result of a process.

First introduced at [[Kardar - 1.4 The Second Law]].
