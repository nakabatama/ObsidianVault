The zeroth law of thermodynamics states that if two systems are separately in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.

It implies the existence of an [[Empirical Temperature|empirical temperature]] that is shared by systems in thermal equilibrium.

First introduced at [[Kardar - 1.2 The Zeroth Law]].
