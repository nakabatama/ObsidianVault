Examples
pressure and volume for fluid; surface tension and area for a film; tension and length for a wire; electric field and polarization for a dielectric.

The state functions are only well defined when the system is in equilibrium. That is the system does not appreciably change with time over the observation times.

The state functions are described by the laws of thermodynamics, which were induced from empirical observation. The laws can also be derived from more fundamental theories like classical or quantum mechanics.
